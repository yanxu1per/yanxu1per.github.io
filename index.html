Primary Research

Damn, I am scared

That was a beautiful evening, we conducted our street interview on homeless community. For their good, we bought fruit and water to treat them. Of course we were rejected for several times and when we arrived at homeless shlter area, we were so scared that no one wanted to enter so all of us stood outside, deciding what to do. But all efforts paid off, different from online interview, our team got very authentic feedback, revealed many unknowns, and felt familiar with this community.Our interview was based on a set of well-designed questions concerning with their basic information, life and attitudes towards status quo.

We grouped the primary needs and observations from these people into a few specific categories: Health, Loss, Technology, Housing, Jobs, Aid, Future, Nutrition, Water, and Substance use. There are large needs in every one of these areas that could each be explored individually in an entire research study. For our insights we hope to focus on those things directly affecting nutrition, from which all of these things are interrelated. We believe that if you were to break down the main barriers currently that are preventing homeless populations from having access to nutritional food, it would fall into education, money, and motivation. Some people are just unaware of what food is best for them to be consuming, but generally this maybe is not the primary reason why there are nutritional problems. Money is obviously a huge factor, but many of these people had enough funds to potentially buy healthy options to eat that they don’t purchase. Ultimately we believe that the biggest current barrier towards nutrition, one that may be hard to understand and analyze because it is so completely interwoven into the complex fabric of reasons that they are currently homeless, is the lack of motivation to live a better and healthier life. Many of these people have given up on that idea that they should pursue the american dream or the pursuit of a long and healthy life. There seems to be a general atmosphere of living life in the present, eating what currently seems best or most fulfilling in that moment.

Prototype

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

We designed the whole work flow of our mobile application by paper prototype, it serves as a usability testing technique and helps us to communicate ideas. Generally speaking, the different screens are sketched prior to the testing. Typical use cases are then created and the users are requested to try and perform them by interacting with the prototype. Based on where they press or select, the human computer will change or alter the prototype by introducing whole screens or snippets, depending on how the system is intended to react. As you will notice, sometimes the facilitator and the human computer is the same person, thus creating the wrong impression that the human computer is speaking during the usability testing session. It is very important that these sessions are well documented using a video camera in order to be able to assess user reactions after the test.
